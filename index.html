Why the new 'BeReal' social media is a great concept, but how long will it last?

By Joseph Nagy

Fri June 3, 2022

The new photo-sharing app called BeReal is not like any other social media platform. It's goal and entire brand revolves around authenticity, and trying to limit the 'fakeness' most social media platforms like instagram contain. It does this by only allowing users to post a picture once a day at a random time, and will provide you with 2 minutes to take the picture. There offers no filters or editng features, so what friends and other users end up seeing is the real you in your daily life.

This app is so popular right now because of how it puts everyone on the same level, there are no 'influencers' creating trends or competing for the most followers. It stops people from comparing their own lives to these random influencers who make you think your life is lame in comparison and instead lets people focus on their friends lives and their own.

This simple, realistic feel this app offers is like a breath of fresh air compared to other socials, but how long until that freshness no longer appeals to people? How long can simple photos of people honestly capture peoples attention for? There are currently a large lack of typical features most platforms offer such as SMS messaging, groupchats, profile customization, looking at old BeReals, etc. This limits the capabilities of the app and how long people can use it for. I personally go on it once a day after I make my post to look through my friends posts. I get that this simplicity is part of the apps brand and purpose, but this stunts the apps growth for future expansions/additions, which makes it, in my opinion, have a short life span.

Key Design Principles for making a great Municipal Website

By Joseph Nagy

Sat June 4, 2022

Municipal Websites are typically, very large and have a lot of content that needs to be covered and organized. The most important thing to think about when designing one of these websites is to only display elements that contribute to the users needs. There are all kinds of different users that visit Municipal sites; government officials, citizens of the township or city, etc. They're purposes for visiting the site may differ, but they all most likely are not there to just browse or explore, they are there to find a specific document, form, ordinance, etc. With this in mind, it becomes crucial to make a design that is well structured, navigable, and contains limited visual elements that may crowd the user interface.

I have been designing and building Municipal sites for over a year and have seen a lot of good websites, and a lot of bad websites that are even hard for me to find certain things. So, in hopes of helping developers design these websites more effectively, I have compiled a list of design principles for a successful Municipal site:

  • Consistency - Designs that change throughout the different pages of a site (or within the page) end up confusing the user and causing them to feel less familiar/confident in using the interface. Give users control by designing repeatable patterns.
  • Smart Content Organization - Organizing content will most likely be the most time consuming part in the design process. Make sure to not overcrowd the navigation with too many pages, especially if some of those pages are similar enough to be grouped together. It is always a good idea to organize content like a tree. The deeper a user is in the 'tree' the more specific the navigation becomes. For example, the main navigation bar could have an option for 'Departments', and inside 'Departments' are subpages for 'Assessor', 'Treasurer', etc, and inside 'Treasurer' could be a page to pay for property taxes. It is essential to not bombard the user with each specific page that exists on the site in the main navigation bar.
